Midnight Highrise

A Neon-Soaked Parkour Escape

Level Design • First-Person Parkour • Unreal Engine
By Tony Grollmes • December 2025

Level Story & Setting

A dense, neon-soaked city during a heavy nighttime rainstorm. The player wakes in a cramped basement maintenance room beneath a corporate district and must ascend through hidden service corridors, rooftops, and high-rise buildings to reach the Skyline Tower rooftop before security systems lock down.

  • Emphasizes verticality and constant upward movement.
  • Mixture of tight interior passages and exposed rooftop segments.
  • Wall-running and wall-clinging as core survival tools.

Objectives

Primary Objective: Escape the basement and reach the Skyline Tower rooftop using parkour movement.

Optional Challenges:

  • Data Collection – Explore risky alternate routes.
  • Time Trial – Complete the run within 3 minutes.
  • Perfect Run – Reach the rooftop with zero deaths from falls.

Stressors & Challenges

Long-Fall Risk: Constant fall danger from basement to exposed rooftops; missed jumps result in lethal drops.

Hazardous Floors: Electrified panels and unstable grates push players toward vertical solutions.

Soft Time Pressure: A 3-minute soft timer nudges players toward high-risk shortcuts and aggressive routing.

Level Progression & Pacing

  • Lower Level: Basic jumps, introductory wall-runs, short clings (low intensity).
  • Street Rooftops: Longer gaps, first major fall exposure, light branching (moderate intensity).
  • Rooftop Cluster: Chained sequences, layered hazards, high consequences (high intensity).
  • Skyline Tower: The hardest sequences and final climb to rooftop (climactic intensity).

Custom Level Mechanics

Wall-Run: Horizontal runs along marked surfaces to cross gaps, bypass hazards, and chain movement between platforms.

Wall-Cling: Short-duration clings after jumps or wall-runs, giving brief pause points for reorientation before losing grip.

Visual Direction

  • Neon, vertical, and rainy night atmosphere.
  • High-contrast cityscape with bright signage cutting through the dark.
  • Glowing billboards and wet reflective rooftops to reinforce height and risk.
  • Stacked architecture that pushes extreme sense of depth and verticality.

Design Inspiration

  • Prince of Persia – Parkour mechanics, wall-clinging, and vertical progression.
  • Titanfall 2 – High-speed wall-running and neon urban energy.

Ready to Ascend?

Midnight Highrise is designed as a focused vertical parkour challenge that blends precise movement, route risk-reward, and a tense neon-soaked escape fantasy.
